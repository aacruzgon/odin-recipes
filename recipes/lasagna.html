Lasagna Recipe

Homepage

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide flat pasta sheets with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel (or ricotta-based cheese mixture), and shredded cheese, typically mozzarella and Parmesan. To prepare it, begin by cooking a meat sauce—usually ground beef or pork sautéed with onions and garlic—then simmer it with crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and Italian herbs like oregano and basil. In parallel, prepare the béchamel by melting butter, whisking in flour to make a roux, then gradually adding milk to form a smooth, thick sauce. Alternatively, a mixture of ricotta cheese, egg, and spinach is used in some regional or vegetarian variations.

To assemble, spread a layer of sauce on the bottom of a baking dish, then add a layer of lasagna noodles (boiled or oven-ready), followed by more sauce, a layer of béchamel or ricotta, and a sprinkle of shredded cheese. Repeat these layers, finishing with a generous topping of cheese. The lasagna is then baked at around 375°F (190°C) for 40–50 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling. After resting for about 10–15 minutes to set, it’s ready to be sliced and served.

Ingridients

Meat Sauce

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz / 796 mL) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for sautéing)

Ricotta Cheese Filling

  • 1 ½ cups (375 g) ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • (Optional) 1 cup chopped spinach, cooked and drained

Béchamel Sauce (Optional)

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups milk (preferably warm)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg

For Assembly

  • 9–12 lasagna noodles (regular or oven-ready)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Extra grated Parmesan cheese (for topping)

Assembly & Baking Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions if you're not using oven-ready noodles. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the meat sauce: In a large skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the chopped onion and garlic until translucent. Add ground meat and cook until browned. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20–30 minutes.
  4. Prepare the ricotta filling: In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan, salt, and pepper. Mix well. Add spinach if using.
  5. (Optional) Prepare the béchamel sauce: In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Whisk in flour to make a roux. Slowly add warm milk while whisking until smooth and thick. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  6. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom.
  7. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  8. Spread a layer of ricotta mixture (or béchamel) over the noodles.
  9. Top with a layer of meat sauce and a sprinkle of shredded mozzarella.
  10. Repeat layers (noodles, ricotta or béchamel, meat sauce, mozzarella) until you reach the top of the dish, finishing with a final layer of sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.
  11. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  12. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15–20 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  13. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
